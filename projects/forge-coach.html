Innovation Forge

A hands-on leadership simulation that puts healthcare executives through a rotating command structure, a Lego bridge-building crisis, and a live AI coaching agent — all in 60 minutes.

4.5/5 Participant Engagement
4.66/5 Likelihood to Apply Skills
4.34/5 Overall Satisfaction
80+ Director-Level Participants
2 Concurrent Sessions

The Challenge

Prisma Health's 2025 Leadership Symposium needed an activity that went beyond typical team-building. The Learning and Development Team wanted something that would put director-level and above participants in real decision-making pressure while also introducing them to AI as a practical leadership tool.

The challenge was designing an experience that felt urgent and immersive without becoming chaotic — and that taught prompt engineering skills without ever mentioning prompt engineering.

Solution

  • Designed a Lego bridge construction scenario framed as a medical transport emergency, where teams had to restore critical routes between facilities under a strict 20-minute build clock with limited pieces and a scoring system that penalized inefficiency.
  • Built a rotating leadership system that cycled participants through Lead Designer, Lead Builder, and Builder roles every three minutes — creating six to seven leadership transitions per team and forcing rapid knowledge transfer that mirrors real succession planning.
  • Developed Forge Coach, a custom AI agent deployed through Microsoft CoPilot Studio, serving as both a structural engineering advisor and a leadership coach. Access was limited to the Lead Designer each cycle, making AI consultation a strategic resource rather than a free-for-all.
  • Stress-tested the agent against other generative AI tools and a calibrated team review process, discovering that pure Socratic questioning frustrated participants under time pressure. Pivoted to a hybrid approach that balanced guiding questions with targeted direct guidance.
  • Produced a full facilitator guide, condensed one-page day-of script, printed role briefs, river template workspace, warehouse visit tracker, and scoring guide — all print-friendly and designed for facilitators with no prior technical background.
Real-World Problem Solving

A five-minute CoPilot outage occurred during the live event — entirely outside our control and unrelated to the agent's design. Because contingency protocols were built into the facilitator guide from the start, the team pivoted to non-AI delivery without disrupting the session. Attendance also exceeded registration across both sessions, requiring real-time facilitation adjustments on the floor. Both situations were handled without participant impact.
