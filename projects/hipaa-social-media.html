HIPAA in Practice: Social Media & Patient Privacy

A scenario-based interaction placing the learner in the role of a Compliance Officer reviewing real social media posts for HIPAA violations

Compliance training on HIPAA privacy often stops at definitions — what PHI is, what the rules require — without asking team members to apply that knowledge in realistic situations. This interaction was designed to close that gap. Rather than selecting from a list of answers, learners click directly on the words or phrases that constitute violations inside a realistic social media post.

Built as a custom HTML block for Articulate Rise 360, deployable as a standalone demo or embedded directly in an eLearning course.

Privacy & HIPAA

Social Media & Patient Privacy

Let's apply what you learned using a popular medium of communication: social media. Healthcare team members use social media in different ways. Some share personal experiences after work. Others post about teamwork, long shifts, or the challenges of working in healthcare.

Even when posts are well-intentioned, patient information can be shared unintentionally. Comments, replies, and added details can also create risk, even if the original post seemed appropriate.

Your Task

In the following scenarios, place yourself in the role of a Compliance Officer reviewing social media posts and comments. Your task is to recognize when information crosses the line and identify what puts patient privacy at risk.

Privacy & HIPAA

Social Media & Patient Privacy

Read the following social media posts to identify if the post is HIPAA compliant. Click on the words or phrases that violate HIPAA. To succeed, you must correctly identify all HIPAA violations. If there are no violations, select the No Violations Present button.
